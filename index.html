About Me

I'm a Master's student in Computer Engineering / Artificial Intelligence at Sharif University of Technology. Under the supervision of Mahdieh Soleymani, I research on the factors toward broad AI, especially Continual Learning in which an agent tries to mimic the human learning behavior by accumulating knowledge in a lifelong fashion.

Previously, I received my bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering / Digital Systems Design in 2019. My BSc project, supervised by Mahdi Shabany and Zahra Kavehvash, was about employing AI algorithms to a Millimeter-Wave body scanner for automatic concealed object detection. Currently, I work as a senior AI researcher at Basir Company to further develop this technology.

My ultimate research goal is to help the community in pushing the borders of AI toward human-level capabilities. For this purpose, the intelligent agent has to overcome the practical difficulties it encounters in real-life problems such as insufficient labeled datasets or the shift in the data distribution.

My Research Interests:

Despite the remarkable success of deep learning in many problems, there is still a long way to reach human-level capabilities. During the years of my academic study, I have earned many valuable experiences in the field of Artificial Intelligence and this gives me a unique perspective to face new challenges. My B.Sc. project proved to me that state-of-the-art techniques still greatly suffer when they are used in real-world problems. These algorithms not only require an expensive task-specific labeled dataset to train on, but they are vulnerable to distribution shifts, lack explainability, and are weak against adversarial attacks. In my Master’s education, I was focused on Continual Learning as a solution to combat data availability and memory constraints that an intelligent agent encounters. Now, I see my main objective to help the community in pushing the boundaries of AI, and for this reason, I have investigated a wide range of research areas including Meta-Learning, Self-Supervised, Semi-Supervised, Statistical Learning, Domain Adaptation, Neuroscience, Brain-Inspired AI, Explainable AI, and ML Security & Robustness. I see all of these topics as the tools that seek to bring AI to its highest point for use in practical applications.
